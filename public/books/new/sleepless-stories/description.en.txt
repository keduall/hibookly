Book Description
A bedtime picture book that embraces a child's night

The anxiety of a sleepless night is not Su-a's problem alone. The owl, the rabbit, and the bear are all spending the night awake for their own reasons. Together with her animal friends, Su-a sets out to find sleep, wandering, asking, and sharing their feelings. Where did sleep run away to? Sleep can feel like something we must work hard to find, but when we think about it, sleep comes naturally. This story may ultimately be a journey toward one simple sentence: "It is okay not to try so hard."

Sleepless Stories is a wonderful picture book both as a bedtime story and as a book that comforts anxious children. What makes it special is that it does not explain the message, "The answer we seek is not far away"; instead, it lets readers experience it. With the gentle softness of a children's picture book and a story structure felt through the body, it naturally guides readers toward that realization.
