Book Description
On the day of the College Scholastic Ability Test, an accident at an intersection twists one girl's future in an instant. Na Young-ri, a model student with top internal grades and an almost certain path to Seoul National University, misses the exam and is forced to face her father's coma. Soon afterward, she receives an unbelievable offer: spend another senior year in high school in place of a conglomerate chairman's daughter, then take the exam for her.

The copycat girl accepts an offer she cannot refuse.
On academic elitism

Copycat Girl looks directly at the bare face of Korean society, where academic elitism and the myth of meritocracy are intertwined. Texty's current-affairs fiction series Side Mirror was created to look closely at social problems we have witnessed but too easily forget. This work asks how the seemingly fairest system, college entrance exams, is in fact shaped by class and capital, and what kind of cliff-edge choices individuals are forced to make within it.

After the accident, Young-ri moves from one part-time job to another to cover hospital bills and living expenses. Because she missed the exam, both early and regular admissions automatically reject her, and three years of achievement collapse overnight. Then the secretary of Chairman Song, whom her father served, comes to find her. Young-ri looks exactly like Song's daughter Cho-rom, and her admission to Seoul National University had seemed certain. That makes her the only person suited for the dangerous project of taking the exam by proxy.
Young-ri, who has never even jaywalked, refuses outright. But reality keeps pressing in: mounting medical bills, the landlord's demand for a higher deposit, and her father's emergency condition. In the end, Young-ri decides to enter the exam room under another name. Chairman Song demands not only a high test score, but also that she join Haneul, the study group of top students at Daemyeong High School, so Cho-rom can enter upper-class society and build ties with children from powerful families. Thus Young-ri's "copycat project" begins.
Through this journey of imitation, the lives of Young-ri and Cho-rom become increasingly entangled, cracks appear inside the world of power, and the price of choice arrives deeper and heavier than expected. Beyond the provocative setup of a proxy exam, Copycat Girl is a psychological thriller and social mystery that pushes the question "Whose opportunity is imitation?" to the very end. It also asks whether our own subjecthood as objects remains intact.

Author Bio
The author began publishing after receiving the Kim Yu-jeong New Writer Literary Award in 2022. In the same year, the author was selected for the Korea Creative Content Agency's emerging story writer program and published the first full-length novel, Hwagwongwi Stationery. Other works include the SF collection The Door of Morpheus, the children's novels The Strange Story of the Signless Stationery Store and Again, Jeong Da-un, and contributions to several anthologies including Juvenile Offender, The Villain Is You, Is There an Ending?, and 1945: Before This World Disappears. Hwagwongwi Stationery has been published overseas, and the short story Portland Officetel, included in All Our Relationships Are Everyone's Business, was optioned for a television drama.
